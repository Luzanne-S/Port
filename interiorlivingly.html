Interior Livingly

Project Background

Interior Livingly is a blog that provides readers with short, easy to read blog posts that encompasses the latest trends that can be used within their individual spaces. The blog started on instagram with quick and easy to follow posts but wants to differentiate itself from other interior design blogs providing a little more to help readers create their own space. The blog wants to provide an AR experience to their readers. A website would be built that has blog posts and the ability for users to open up an AR function to place items from our catalogue into their spaces.

Research Objective

The objective is to explore the thought process of the readers when opting to redo a space and how they envision adding furnishings and furnishing to their space.

Desired Outcomes

  • ❖ Learning what the thought process is of the reader when they decorate their space
  • ❖ What type of experience do they want to have when making purchases
  • ❖ How to make the experience as easy as possible

Research Activites

Activity 1: Online Research

Timeline 3 days

Examining blogs online: seeing what are other blogs doing

Key questions to ask:

  • ❖ What makes a blog popular
  • ❖ Who has a interior design blog in South Africa
  • ❖ What makes interior livingly different
  • ❖ How are decor companies selling online
  • ❖ How are AR apps changing shopping behaviour
  • ❖ How many AR apps are available to South Africans

Activity 2: Qualitative Research

Timeline 2 weeks

Conduct interviews and polls online with readers and potential readers

Key questions to ask:

  • ❖ What encourages them to change their interior spaces
  • ❖ How do they prefer to shop
  • ❖ How do they currently feel about their experience while shopping for furniture
  • ❖ What influences them to buy a particular item

Participant Mix

  • ❖ All genders
  • ❖ About 10-30 participants
  • ❖ Residing inside of South Africa
  • ❖ Age group (20-60) - people with disposable income
  • ❖ Readers of interior design blogs and current readers
  • ❖ Potential readers - people on instagram and twitter who tag interior design or follow pages of furniture companies online

Screening and Interview Questions

Findings

All findings are more detailed and have been summarised for the portfolio

Findings From Online Research

AR is the future of customer shopping experience.Allowing customers to 'try before you buy' without actually walking into a store.

Web AR is a simplified version of AR with its pro's and con's but allows for a broader range of users through web searches.

There are not as many African/ South African based interior designer or furniture stores that provide AR experience to customers.

Findings From Particpants

Once all the data was collected I then came to my conclusions was by splitting the data so that it could be easily measured by:

Quantitative results are from close ended questions; yes and no or given participants options to choose from.

Qualitative results are from the patterns of responses from participants substantiating their responses. I could then group keywords and deduce themes from the findings.

Overall readers and potential readers of the blog came to a few conclusions:

  • ❖ They want easy and affordable/ free advice to style their homes.
  • ❖ They would like a shopping experience without leaving their homes but being assured of the product's accurate description.
  • ❖ They do not want to commit to apps that takes up space on their phones or any fees associated with the app.

Persona

I created a user persona that matches the average, current reader of the blog.

Planning

I first decided on what type of application to choose from which was: a website with a Web AR feature. The website would have:

  • ❖ The ability to host the blog articles and Web AR feature
  • ❖ Easy to access for new users
  • ❖ Less commitment, user would not have to download an app
  • ❖ No app store fees to host it

I decided to make it easy to use as most users either: do not use AR or have a low level knowledge of AR.

Easy navigation for users, without too many features to complicate the user's experience.

Photographs of furniture items would be converted from images to 3d models through the use of blender.

Wireframe

Desktop Wireframe

The low fidelity desktop wireframe begins on the landing page of the site and branches off into other pages. Users will be able to access most of the site with the exception of the web AR feature.

  • Landing Page
  • About Page
  • Blog Page
  • Blog Article Page with advertisements on the side panels
  • Catalogue Page
  • Catalogue Modal
  • Contact Page

Mobile Wireframe

To access the web AR feature, the user would need to use a mobile/tablet.

  • Home Page, has a mobile navbar. The user can scroll down, each section of the homepage provides the user with a brief description about the website.
  • About Page, the user is given a more detailed description of how the website came to be. The About page provides call to action buttons to the blog and catalogue pages.
  • Blog Page, has the current blog posts. Each post has a brief summary and link to the full article. The user can then click on an article and will then be taken to relevant page.
  • Article Page, is the full article with images and convienent breadcrumbs at the top of the page for the user to navigate back to either the home page or blog main page.
  • Catalogue Page, displays furniture items for the user to explore, once a user clicks on an item then a modal will open up.
  • Catalogue Modal, the modal has the name of the item , description, price, link to the manufacturer's website and button to open up the web AR feature.
  • Web AR Page, when the button is clicked the user can then scan their room and the item will be placed into the room.
  • Contact Page, once the user has been through the website, they are most likely to open up the contact page. Which has a user friendly contact form that is short and easy to fill out.

Reflection

The next step is to pitch the idea to furniture stores and manufacturers to be able to advertise their products on the site.
