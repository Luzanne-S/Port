Swad Foods

Swad Foods is a newly established weekly meal catering company, that focuses on making homemade Indian vegan and vegetarian meals. The company currently has not opened its doors yet. The company wants to reach out to customers through channels that they may already have access to: Whatsapp, Facebook, Instagram and later through having a web presence.

The current goal is to have the resources available (brand identity, digital menu and a means of communication) for customers to have access to, then have a website to reach more customers.

First Phase

Understand the needs of the client and do some background research.

Brief

Use the current logo to draw inspiration from;

  • ❖ Colours: red and yellow
  • ❖ Style: easy to navigate, not cluttered, oriental feel with modern elements and lines

The website needs to provide images and information of the meals. An additional FAQ page for any questions customers may have.

Competitors Websites

Swad Foods' current competitors are: Sam's Kitchen and Bex the Chef because,they offer weekly meals and they have an easy means of contact through; Whatsapp and social media sites.

These website's layout and style are simple and not cluttered. For these reasons the website's structure appeals to the owner of Swad, who would like to incorporate the layout but add their own uniqueness.

Second Phase

The second phase would be creating wireframes for the website.


Low Fidelity Wireframes

I created a low fidelity wireframes to show the owner how the potential website's layout would be.

I created a one page desktop wireframe that directs the user vertically;

  • Header Section
  • About Section
  • Services Section
  • Weekly Menu
  • Meet the Chef
  • Tesimonials and Footer
  • Seperate page for Frequently Asked Questions

I then created a wireframe for a smaller screens (mobile) viewports. This viewport differs by having a hamburger menu navbar that extends when clicked and allows for a vertical view rather than horizontal

Third Phase

High Fidelity Wireframe

Once the low fidelity layout was approved by Swad's owner. I then created high fidelity wireframes. I kept the styling simple but still added colour to differentiate the website from the competitor websites.

The feedback I recieved was that the white font in the yellow boxes needed to be changed to a navy blue. Once I made the changes I then sent the images to the client, I then received the feedback that the design was approved.
