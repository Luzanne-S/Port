Maji Ndogo Water Crisis

Maji Ndogo is a fictional country that mirrors real-world challenges. With a database of 60,000 records, meticulously collected by a team of engineers, field workers, scientists, and analysts. The purpose is to piece together the puzzle of the water crisis that the country is facing. The objective is to find solutions to the water crisis.

Project Objective

Analyse the data to find patterns and solutions to the water crisis facing the country of Maji Ndogo. Then present the findings to stakeholders.

  • ✓  Creating databases and querying the data with SQL
  • ✓  Identify patterns and indescrepencies within data

Summary of Analysis

The national water access project in Maji Ndogo, spanning from 2022 to 2027, faced a series of challenges and successes. Surveyors' reports revealed instances of corruption within the project, prompting the utilization of SQL to query data and enabling thorough investigations. This effort led to the criminal charges against corrupt officials and surveyors, ensuring accountability and transparency in the project's execution. Simultaneously, insights from engineers highlighted areas that required improvement, providing a comprehensive understanding of the necessary enhancements to positively impact the lives of Maji Ndogo residents.



The project's overarching plan successfully achieved its primary objectives by increasing water access and mitigating issues such as long queues and the potential for criminal activities. However, financial constraints emerged as a significant hurdle, with costs surpassing the initially projected budget. Vendor overcharging and limitations in infrastructure to reach rural areas impeded the team's efficiency in accessing and completing projects within stipulated timelines. Despite these challenges, the project can be deemed a success, marking a groundbreaking initiative for the country. Its significance lies not only in the enhanced water accessibility for residents but also as a learning opportunity for planners and stakeholders. The lessons derived from this pioneering project will contribute to reducing the likelihood of exceeding budgets in future endeavors and improving overall project management practices.
