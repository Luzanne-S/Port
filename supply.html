Summary of Analysis

The in-depth analysis of the Just-In-Time supply chain identified; Mauritania, Costa Rica, Tunisia, and Lesotho as pivotal contributors to overall profits over a three year period. Cleats emerged as consistent profit generators and mainstay in inventory throughout the year, but during the months of October, November, and December, a shift in profits towards gardening equipment, books, crafts, and women's apparel was evident. Examining the impact of order quantity on costs and profits revealed that an optimal order quantity remains elusive.

However, an average order quantity of 1895 was identified as a compromise that minimizes costs while ensuring adequate stock levels. Shipment delays, averaging 21.43 days, were noted across two warehouses, with the smaller warehouse in Puerto Rico experiencing a slightly higher delay at 26.47 days on average.



To enhance supply chain efficiency, strategic improvements were proposed, including consumer research in low-order countries to align marketing with local buying trends, grouping order fulfillment inventory by product categories, and stocking warehouses accordingly. Additionally, a recommendation was made to reduce inventory for out-of-season and low-selling products, focusing on specialized stocking strategies for sport-related and men's clothing.

An actionable plan for the future involves increasing stock levels for gardening equipment, books, crafts, and women's apparel specifically during October, November, and December. Further steps include a detailed product trend analysis for predictive demand, categorizing products for each warehouse to minimize delays, conducting warehouse inspections to address potential hindrances to order fulfillment, and a comprehensive review of current marketing strategies to ensure adaptability to global changes.

In conclusion, this supply chain analysis has not only unveiled areas of profitability but also provided a roadmap for strategic improvements. By addressing specific challenges and seizing opportunities, we are poised for a more resilient and responsive supply chain in the dynamic global market.
